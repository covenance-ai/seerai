How seerai works

The essentials — what it does, what you get from it, and what safeguards sit around your people's AI-assistant data.

About seerai

What is seerai?

seerai is an observer layer for the AI-assistant conversations happening across your organization — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot, internal LLM apps, any of it. It turns scattered chat logs into organizational insight (which tools pay off, where AI accelerates work, what your people are actually doing with it) without exposing any one employee's conversations to their manager.

It sits alongside whatever AI tools your people already use. You don't change how they work; you gain visibility into what's happening.

What you get

What decisions does seerai help us make?
  • Cost & ROI — which AI subscriptions pay for themselves, which are dead weight, which cost center to charge.
  • Analytics — where across the org AI accelerates work vs. slows it down, which tasks land and which get abandoned.
  • Insights — cross-team signals (who is suddenly asking security's questions?), paygrade alignment, negative-ROI and prevented-harm patterns.
  • Company knowledge base — a living corpus of problems your people have actually solved using AI, useful for onboarding and reference.
What do our employees get from it?

Every employee has a private view — their own sessions, costs, usage patterns, coaching nudges — visible only to them.

This matters: it makes seerai something your people benefit from first, not something done to them. Adoption follows.

Privacy & compliance

Who sees what?
  • The employee — their own AI sessions in full.
  • Exec / manager in your company — aggregates, insights, and the company knowledge base. Never an individual's raw session.
  • seer.ai platform staff — only items your company has explicitly flagged for support. No ambient access.
Can a manager read an individual employee's AI conversations?

Depends on the privacy posture you choose per organization:

  • Privacy mode ON (default for EU / works-council contexts) — no. Only the employee themselves. Aggregates enforce a minimum cohort size so small teams don't indirectly identify anyone.
  • Privacy mode OFF — managers in the same company can view individual sessions, like any ordinary productivity tool.

You control this in Settings → Privacy. Toggling the setting is itself audited.

GDPR, data residency, and exit?
  • seerai acts as processor under GDPR Art. 28; your company is the controller. A DPA signed at onboarding defines the scope.
  • Data is hosted in GCP europe-west1 (EU). No cross-border transfers.
  • Data subject rights (access, erasure) are supported per employee.
  • At contract end, your tenant's data — including the derived knowledge base — is deleted.
